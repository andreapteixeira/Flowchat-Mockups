Enable White Label Mode

Alright, let's get your FlowChat platform all set up with your own branding, so you can share this amazing tool with your clients. Follow these simple steps:

Step 1

Log in to your account and access your account information. You'll find the White Label option by clicking on your name in the top right corner of the page and selecting it from the dropdown menu. If you prefer, you can also just click here to open it in another tab.

Step 2

Enable White Label. Once you've found the White Label option, you'll need to enable it. This will reveal all the settings available for this feature, so you can start customizing your platform to your liking.

Step 3

Now it's time to check out all the cool settings available for the White Label feature. Don't forget to mark this lesson as complete and follow us to the next one for more customization options. Let's get started!

Define Your Product Settings

Within the White Label Settings, you have the ability to personalize your brand by setting your white label product name, sub-domain and defining your contact email, all of which will be visible to your clients.

Step 1

Display your own Product Name. To make your brand stand out, you can replace FlowChat's name with your own product name. Just enable the option and type in your name (try to keep it under 15 characters if possible).

Step 2

Custom Sub-domain. You can also use your own custom sub-domain instead of the flowchat.com domain. Type in your sub-domain and then update your DNS by adding a CNAME record pointing to www.flowchat.com. Once that's done, let us know so we can enable your sub-domain on our end. For more details, check out the next lesson.

Step 3

Custom Support Email. If you want clients to be able to contact you directly, enter your custom support email in this field. Otherwise, your account email will be used.

Step 4

Custom Failed Payment Email. Similarly, you can set a custom failed payment email to receive notifications from the payment platform about any failed renewals. If you don't want to use a custom email, your account email will be used instead.

Step 5

Custom Privacy Policy Link. If you have a custom Privacy Policy enter the link in this field. If the field is left blank, our default text for Privacy Policy will be displayed.

Step 6

Custom Terms & Conditions Link. If you have a custom Terms & Conditions enter the link in this field. If the field is left blank, our default text for Terms & Conditions will be displayed.

Step 7

GoHighLevel Product Name. If you have White Label of GoHighLevel, put in here the product name so it will show this name instead of GoHighLevel on our 3rd Party Integrations page.

Step 8

Enable Affiliate Area for Affiliates. If you don't want your members to access the Affiliate resource area or share your platform with a personal link for commission, turn on this option.

Step 9

Show Product Guide. If you don't want your members to access the product guide, turn off this option.

Step 10

Show Support Links. If you don't want your members to access the support links and FAQs, turn off this option.

Step 11

Update your changes. Remember to always save your changes!

Setup Your Own Domain

Here's a detailed guide on how to set up your own sub-domain to work with your white-labeled platform.

Step 1

Custom Sub-Domain. Create a custom sub-domain. Just type in the name you want to use for your sub-domain in the White Label Settings. For example, you can use "app.yourdomain.com".

Step 2

Update Your changes. Once you've entered your sub-domain, don't forget to save your changes by clicking the update button at the bottom right.

Step 3

Access your domain's DNS Manager. Now you need to access your domain control panel, where you can manage the DNS records for your domain. You can do this through your domain registrar account or on your server control panel, depending on how you have your domain configured.

Step 4

Add a CNAME record. In your domain's DNS Manager, add a CNAME record to your chosen sub-domain and point it to www.flowchat.com. Once you've made this change, make sure to save it.

Step 5

Contact us. After you've updated your sub-domain, please let us know through Slack or our support email support@flowchat.com so we can update it on our end. We'll let you know once your sub-domain is ready to use. And that's it!

Branding Settings

You can add your own logo to the FlowChat platform replacing our brand with your own. For that you need to have your logo image available and follow these simple steps:

Step 1

Upload Your Logo. Now, let's upload your logo. A png file with transparent background would work best, and we recommend a size of around 300x90 or higher. Your logo will pop up on the left side panel, the login page, and the subscription page.

Step 2

Upload Your Favicon. Time to upload your favicon - this one will show up on the browser tab. Again, a png file with a transparent background will do the trick, and we suggest a size of 128x128 pixels or higher.

Step 3

Members Area Left Sidebar Color. Choose a background color for the sidebar on your members' area. This will help create a consistent look and feel throughout your site.

Step 4

Login Page Background Color. Select a background color for your login page, as well as other pages outside of the members' area, such as the privacy policy, subscription process, and pricing page.

Step 5

Pricing Page Text. You can also customize the text on your pricing page to make it more appealing to potential customers. If you don't have anything specific in mind, don't worry - we've got you covered with a default text.

Step 6

Update your changes. Finally, don't forget to hit the "Update" button to save your changes. That way, your platform and login page will be looking great in no time!

Support Box Settings

In the Support Box Settings, you can enable a support box that you can use to display some helpful information to your members, or even create a list of upcoming events that they might be interested in.

Step 1

Enable Support Box. To get started, you'll need to enable the support box. If you change your mind later, you can always disable it.

Step 2

Support Box Editor. . Once you've enabled it, you can start customizing it using the Support Box Editor. This will give you a preview of what the support box will look like, and you can make changes to the various information areas by hovering over them.

Step 3

Support Box main text. One of the most important areas to customize is the Support Box Main Text. Here, you can create a personalized message for your members using rich text, which means you can add links, format the text, or even throw in a few emojis if you like.

Step 4

Support Box Image. If you really want to make your support box stand out, you can even add an image! Just make sure it's a square image with dimensions of 250 x 250 pixels.

Step 5

Name and Job Title. And don't forget to add your name and job title in the designated fields. This helps give your members an idea of who they're talking to.

Step 6

Brand Logo. After that, you can add a logo to the support box! It's best to use a PNG format with a transparent background, and the most convenient dimensions are 200 x 50 pixels.

Step 7

List items. Last but not least, you can add a list of events to the support box.

Step 8

Event Details. For each event, you can include a title, subtitle, frequency, start date and time, availability, and button text and URL if you want to provide a link (like a zoom call for example).

Step 9

Support Box Color. To really make it your own, you can select the Support Box Color and choose a background gradient that matches your branding.

Step 10

Update your changes. Once you're happy with your changes, be sure to update them before leaving the settings page. And if you ever want to revert back to the default colors, you can always reset them.

Stripe Integration Settings

In this tutorial, we will show you how to integrate Stripe payments into your platform so that your members can manage their payments and billing. The process involves several steps, which we will explain below.

Step 1

Allow Clients to Update the Billing Info and Payment Methods. If you want your members to manage their payments, turn on this option and configure a Stripe Account (see the lesson on that).

Step 2

Enable Stripe Integration. To enable Stripe integration, you need to go to your platform's settings and select the option to enable this feature. Enabling Stripe integration means that your members can manage their billing through Stripe.

Step 3

Fill in your Stripe Keys and web hook. To complete the integration, you need to fill in your Stripe Keys and webhook information. You can obtain this information from your Stripe account by visiting the following links:

  • Live API Keys: https://dashboard.stripe.com/apikeys
  • Live Webhooks: https://dashboard.stripe.com/webhooks
  • Test API Keys: https://dashboard.stripe.com/test/apikeys
  • Test Webhooks: https://dashboard.stripe.com/test/webhooks

Note that you can include the test credentials for testing purposes only.

Step 4

Choose the product/plan. After enabling Stripe integration and filling in your Stripe Keys and webhook information, you need to choose a product or plan that you have created in Stripe. This will associate the product or plan with your white label product and charge your members accordingly.

Only plans with a monthly interval are valid as offered plans to sub-accounts. You can add products and plans to your Stripe account at https://dashboard.stripe.com/products?active=true.

Step 5

Update your changes. Once you have completed the above steps, don't forget to hit the "Update" button to save your changes.

In conclusion, integrating Stripe payments with your platform is a straightforward process that can greatly benefit your members by allowing them to manage their billing easily. By following the steps outlined in this tutorial, you can successfully integrate Stripe payments into your platform.

Step 3

Update your changes. After completing the above steps, remember to hit the "Update" button to save your changes.

Mailgun Integration Settings

Next, we will establish the method for sending email messages to your members. To achieve this, you will need to integrate a 3rd party service, Mailgun. This service will enable you to send bulk messages using your own domain.

If you haven't done so already, please create an account with Mailgun.

Step 1

Access 3rd Party Integrations. To access the 3rd party integration settings, click on your name located at the top right corner of the screen. From the dropdown menu, select "3rd Party Integrations". Alternatively, you can click here to open the settings in another tab.

Step 2

Enable Mailgun Integration. Enable the integration with Mailgun so you can send email messages with your own custom domain.

Step 3

Paste Your Mailgun API Key. Locate the Mailgun API Key field and paste your API key from Mailgun. After that, click “Connect”. You can find your API Key here: https://app.mailgun.com/app/account/security/api_keys

Step 4

Select a Domain Name. Choose the domain from which you want to send your email messages. You will see a dropdown list of the domains you have set up in Mailgun.

Step 5

Update your changes. Save your changes to ensure that your settings are updated. Don’t forget to do this before moving on to the next step.

In conclusion, enabling your White Label account is a simple process that requires you to contact us through Slack. By following the steps outlined in this tutorial, you can successfully get in touch with Bruno and have your white label domain enabled and your extension deployed.
